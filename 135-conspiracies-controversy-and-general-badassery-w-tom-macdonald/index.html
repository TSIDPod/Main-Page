The question of the day is.... What are y'all so butthurt about?

Well, as we quickly approach the end of 2019, one can only hope that 2020 will be less touchy and bring back the beautiful edge of art that has been sorely lacking this year, due to the fun-sucking stench of hyper political correctness and cancel culture.

It certainly wasn't a total loss of a year tho. Out of the political / entertainment outrage tire fire, we were bless with emergences of boundary pushing art and artists like Dave Chappelle, Joker, and of course, the continual rise of Tom MacDonald.

If you're not familiar with Tom yet, it's time to change that and never look back. For the last few years, rap artist Tom MacDonald has been rapidly rising in popularity in the music scene with his controversial tracks on topics like race ("Straight White Male", "White Boy", "If I was Black"), political correctness ("Politically Incorrect", "Everybody Hates Me", "Buttholes"), and now taking it a step even further into the outrage realms by touching on conspiratorial hot-button topics ("Cloned Rappers", "Trying to Kill Me") saying everything from "9/11 was an inside job" to "the Illuminati is real" to Eminem being a clone since his album, Encore. Long story short, whatever your flavor of outrage, there's a good chance Tom has something in his arsenal to wad your panties too.

But is there more to Tom Macdonald than just an elaborate, talented, button pushing, troll? Does Tom really believe in the things he's talking about or is it all just for those delicious outrage clicks? What's next in store for the hip hop prince of political incorrectness? All that and so much more on today's episode of The System is Down episode 135: Conspiracies, Controversy, and General Badassery w. Tom MacDonald
